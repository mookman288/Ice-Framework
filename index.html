Ice Framework

By PxO Ink

The Ice Framework is a modifiable and reusable system that can provide a basic structure for implementing fluid and accessible websites. This framework isn't designed to provide the most compelling aesthetic experience. Nor is it designed to standardize the web. Instead, utilizing the Ice Framework should provide a clean and scalable experience that delivers DOM content quickly, and then focuses on displaying it in a way that users can appreciate, but not be overwhelmed by. This page represents examples of what can be done with this framework.

Download the latest version. Looking for something lighter? Try Skeleton. Looking for something more robust? Try Foundation.

Grid

Most common grid systems are designed around a standardized 960px maximum width. This is supported by the ever increasing number of 12–16 column layouts. This provides for centered designs that only harness a small portion of large resolutions, while catering to smaller resolutions almost exclusively.

What those grid systems lack is scalability across a plethora of device widths.

Today's devices are quite quirky. They often fail to conform to any type of easily perceived standard, especially one that would satiate the cravings of both designers and developers alike. For instance, some devices will have a horizontal resolution of 720px, but will masquerade themselves as having anywhere between a 360–384px viewport width. This is an act of device engineers trying to provide their users a comfortable experience, based on the physical dimensions of the device itself. Other devices will tote a massive 2560–3840px+ viewport width.

Trying to cater to every single device can waste precious time that would be better spent on providing a more accessible experience. Too often do teams abandon important aspects of the site for the sake of presenting a compelling design. It's time to focus less on pixel perfection, and more on providing a worthwhile and fast experience for users.

Twenty Clear

The Ice Framework is designed around granularity with a base 20 column layout anchored by a maximum 99.88% container reach. The philosophy behind this particular grid is scalability across a wide array of devices, approximate or estimated design, and basic mathematical support. In order for the framework to succeed, it needs to look presentable on as many resolutions as possible while still loading quickly. Therefore, eight common break points were chosen resulting in enough classes to handle almost any situation.

  • column class – left – right – clear – cfx clearfix
  • full – 2160px – ex: full-10 – full-margin-half-10 – full-margin-ends-10-half
    full-margin-(ends | sides | top | bottom | left | right | half)-#(-half)
    full-padding-(ends | sides | top | bottom | left | right | half)-#(-half)
  • ultra – 1680px – ex: ultra-16 – ultra-padding-2
    ultra-margin-(ends | sides | top | bottom | left | right | half)-#(-half)
  • wide – 1440px – ex: wide-4
    wide-margin-(ends | sides | top | bottom | left | right | half)-#(-half)
    wide-padding-(ends | sides | top | bottom | left | right | half)-#(-half)
  • standard – 1000px
    standard-margin-(ends | sides | top | bottom | left | right | half)-#(-half)
    standard-padding-(ends | sides | top | bottom | left | right | half)-#(-half)
  • small – 860px
    small-margin-(ends | sides | top | bottom | left | right | half)-#(-half)
    small-padding-(ends | sides | top | bottom | left | right | half)-#(-half)
  • mobile – 840px
    mobile-margin-(ends | sides | top | bottom | left | right | half)-#(-half)
    mobile-padding-(ends | sides | top | bottom | left | right | half)-#(-half)
  • phone – 480px
    phone-margin-(ends | sides | top | bottom | left | right | half)-#(-half)
    phone-padding-(ends | sides | top | bottom | left | right | half)-#(-half)
  • micro – 320px
    micro-margin-(ends | sides | top | bottom | left | right | half)-#(-half)
    micro-padding-(ends | sides | top | bottom | left | right | half)-#(-half)

This column system, and the responsive system at large, is inverted. Most systems have the class system specifying the smallest break point, expanding upward to the largest break point. Ice Framework, however, works by specifying the largest break point, and then specifying smaller break points as needed.

Example


<div class="cfx">
	<div class="column left full-6 full-padding-ends-1 mobile-8 mobile-padding-ends-1-half">
		Hello World!
	</div>
	<div class="column right full-10 full-padding-ends-1 mobile-12 mobile-padding-ends-1-half">
		Check out clearfix! 
	</div>
</div>

Focus less on pixel perfect design, and estimate design based upon what aesthetically appears and feels right. Conform less to unweidly grids, and embrace granularity.

Prefer a different design? This framework provides a set of variables that can be tuned to a particular design need. During compilation, Sass will re-calculate the grid based on input.

In the future, it might be nice to fork this grid to be based on the golden ratio grid system in Sass.

JavaScript

Some care has been taken to provide a graceful fallback for situations where JavaScript is not available. Elements of the Ice Framework may not function completely without JavaScript. In order to provide a speedy environment, this framework expects most JavaScript to be compiled, minified, and loaded after the DOM has rendered.
